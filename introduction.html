Introduction to Japanese Language


Modern Japanese is written in a mixture of three basic scripts: Kanji — which are Chinese ideographic symbols — as well as Hiragana and Katakana — two phonetic alphabets (syllables).
There are a few thousand Kanji characters, while Hiragana and Katakana have 46 each.

Sentence structure of Japanese language is very similar to that of Hindi or Marathi but different from English. Just look at the below example.

Consider a sentence "I drink water."

English :: I drink Water.

Senetnce structure :: Noun - Verb - Object

Hindi :: मैं पानी पीता हूँ.

Senetnce structure :: Noun - Object - Verb

Marathi :: मी पाणी पितो

Senetnce structure :: Noun - Object - Verb

Japanese :: Watashi wa mizu o nomimasu

Senetnce structure :: Noun - Object - Verb
Watashi- I
Mizu - water
Nomimasu - drink
